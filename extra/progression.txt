Seventh (technically 6.1) Attempt:
I realized the autograder can only fill up one of our memories (so only 1 copy of I-MEM).
And since each RAM cell in CircuitSim is single ported, we would be screwed.
BUT, I made a 4-way L1 Cache that can unconditionally sends our PC+1 to I-MEM,
unconditionally copies to L1, and then reads the other 3 addresses.
This cache is only for the Instruction Memory, but it helps a whole lot!
We technically lost superscalar performance, but at least we run properly on the autograder!

Seventh.1 Attempt:
Fixed L1 Instruction Cache!!! So, actually, you see, not doing a Stall-Fill was part of the issue,
but the rest of it came from an ugly bug where I didn't wrap around.
Say we're at PC=3 (we want PC=4,5,6 next).
That means we're pulling from Bank 4's Address 0, but Banks 1, 2, and 3's Address 1's.
So, we can't feed the same address into all of them. The MMU has to do something.
POW: 1255, FIB: 4785, TWO_SUM: 2076
