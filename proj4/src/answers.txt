Problem-1 (FCFS Scheduler)
---------------------------
No time for stats. You should see a semi linear gain for the first 2.
However, as time goes on, adding more processors doesn't help.
Since you'll be IO limited when you add some large number of processors,

________________________
Problem-2 (Round-Robin)
------------------------
Shortening time slices will initially decrease waiting time.
But, at some point, you'll make them too short and then you'll have too much overhead.
This excessive overhead will cause your effective waiting time to increase
when your time slices are too small. The relationship is initially linear.
Later, it's roughly constant.
The shortest timeslice is not necessarily good because of excessive overhead.

Problem-3 (Preemptive Priority)
--------------------------------
Age them. Lower Priority Processes that have been waiting for a while need to be bumped up in priority.
Typically, this is done by "aging" them. So, the longer they've been waiting,
the higher their priority gets "bumped" over their original priority.
This mitigates the effects of lower priority processes never being run due to
higher priority processes always taking precedence over them.

Problem-4 (The Priority Inversion Problem)
-------------------------------------------
Since P1 is dependent upon the window manager, the Window Manager should actually
temporarily receive a status of High Priority (just like P1) since, for now,
P1 is just an extension of the Window Manager. Then, once the Window Manager is done
with serving P1, it gets demoted again.
